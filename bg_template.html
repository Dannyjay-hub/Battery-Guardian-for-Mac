About Battery Guardian

Battery Guardian is a forensic tool that detects counterfeit, reprogrammed, or spoofed MacBook batteries by analyzing the Texas Instruments gas gauge chip data.

Health Score: A composite 0–100 rating combining capacity, cycle count, and spoof indicators.

Physics Check (Zero Entropy): Verifies that individual cells have natural capacity variance. Spoofed batteries hardcode identical values across all three cells.

Internal Resistance: Compares chemical capacity (Qmax) vs deliverable capacity (FCC). After 30+ cycles, a genuine battery always shows a measurable impedance gap.

Lazy Clone: Checks whether Qmax exactly matches Design Capacity — the most common DataFlash hack to fake 100% health.

DOD0 Calibration: Verifies the depth-of-discharge calibration record is genuine. A DOD0 equal to Design Capacity is fabricated — real calibrations always discharge slightly less.

Clock Integrity: Cross-checks two independent TI chip counters. A discrepancy ≥20% means a counter was reset to hide the battery's real age.

Calibration Paradox: CycleCountLastQmax can never exceed CycleCount. If it does, the cycle counter was reset — physically impossible without tampering.

Chip Origin: A 3-cell MacBook pack cannot drop below 9,000mV (TI CUV floor). A lower MaximumPackVoltage means the chip came from a 2-cell device.

Frozen Clock: TotalOperatingTime must increment continuously. If unchanged in 30+ hours between scans, the firmware counter is disabled or spoofed.
